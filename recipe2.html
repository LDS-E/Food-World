Escondidinho

A Brazilian classic, the escondidinho may seem laborious but it is worth each stage of preparation (which can be distributed on different days to make it easier). It yields beautifully and works as a stand-alone dish.

Escondidinho is an A Brazilian classic What is Escondidinho "- aka little Hidden"? Dish made in the oven, generally consisting of a layer of cassava or potato puree that covers a preparation of dried meat or chicken. The recipe is simple and probably humble: spread a layer of mashed potatoes on a platter and then distribute the jerky/dried meat/sun-dried meat/paçoca, chicken or shrimp on top.

Ingredients :

  • 1 kg of dried meat
  • 2 ripe tomatoes
  • 2 onions
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 5 sprigs of parsley
  • olive oil
  • black pepper to taste
  • 1 kg of peeled cassava
  • 1 cup of milk (tea)
  • 2½ tablespoons of butter
  • 1 cup finely grated curd cheese

Method :

  • Step 1:
  • Cut the dried meat into 7 cm cubes – this is the ideal size for desalting the meat. Wash well under running water and place the cubes in a bowl.
  • Cover the meat cubes with 5 cups (tea) of cold water and leave in the fridge for 12 hours – change the water in the dried meat at least twice during desalting.
  • Drain the water and transfer the meat to the pressure cooker, cover with water until half the volume of the pan is filled, cover and place over high heat. As soon as the pan starts to whistle, lower the heat and let it cook for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare the other ingredients.
  • Peel and finely chop the onions and garlic. Wash, dry and cut the tomatoes in half; discard the seeds and cut each half into small cubes. Wash, dry and roughly chop the parsley.
  • After the cooking time, turn off the heat. Wait for all the pressure to release before opening the pan (if you prefer, lift the valve with a fork to speed up the process).
  • Reserve ½ cup (tea) of the cooking water and transfer the meat to a platter. Discard the excess fat and shred the pieces while still hot with two forks.
  • Place a large frying pan (or saucepan) over medium heat. When heated, drizzle with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, add the onion, season with a pinch of salt and sauté for 2 minutes, until wilted. Add the garlic, tomato and stir for 1 minute.
  • Drizzle with another 2 tablespoons of olive oil, add the shredded meat, season with salt and pepper and sauté for 2 minutes to absorb the flavors. Add the reserved cooking water, parsley and mix well. Transfer to a bowl and reserve for assembly.
  • Step 2:
  • Preheat the oven to 240° C (high temperature).
  • Cut the cassava into large 8 cm slices. Place in the pressure cooker, cover with water, without exceeding the maximum level of ⅔ of the pan, and place over high heat. When it starts to whistle, lower the heat and let it cook for 10 minutes.
  • Turn off the heat and let all the pressure release before opening the pan. Drain the water from the cassava and transfer the pieces to a large pan.
  • Discard the central fiber and, using a vegetable masher, mash the pieces while they are still hot in the pan (if you prefer, mash the cassava with a fork). Add the milk and place the pan over low heat. Stir well with a spatula for about 5 minutes until a very creamy puree forms. Season with salt and mix in the butter.
  • To assemble the escondidinhos: fill 8 individual ramekins with the braised dried meat; cover with the cassava puree and sprinkle with the grated rennet cheese. Bake in the oven for about 20 minutes, or until the cheese melts and turns golden.
  • Remove from the oven and serve immediately.
  • Tip: Hiding in secret may seem like a lot of work, but with planning everything becomes easier. You can prepare the dried meat stew the day before and the puree on the day of assembly, or leave the ramekins assembled in the refrigerator to brown just before serving (in this case, remember to remove the ramekins from the refrigerator while the oven pre-heats).
