Suya Shawarma Sensation: Exploring the Flavors of Nigerian Spicy Beef Suya Wrap

As a child, one of my fondest memories was the time my family took me to a bustling Nigerian street market. The air was filled with enticing aromas of grilled meats and spices, and my senses were instantly captivated. Amidst the vibrant atmosphere, my eyes landed on a food cart adorned with colorful banners that read "Nigerian Spicy Beef Suya Wrap." Excitedly, I tugged at my parents' hands, urging them to try this enticing treat.

We joined the queue, patiently waiting as the vendor skillfully grilled thin slices of marinated beef on a smoking hot grill. The tantalizing scent of the Suya spices filled the air, making my mouth water in anticipation. Finally, it was our turn. The vendor warmly greeted us, his hands expertly assembling the shawarma. He spread a generous layer of garlic sauce on a warm flatbread, then topped it with crisp lettuce, juicy tomatoes, and crunchy onions. And then, the pièce de résistance – he placed a heap of the perfectly grilled Suya beef slices on top. As he rolled the flatbread tightly, the flavors melded together, creating a mouthwatering masterpiece. The first bite was heavenly.

Ingredients :

  • 500 grams beef (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tablespoons ground peanuts or peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons ground ginger
  • 2 tablespoons ground paprika
  • 1 tablespoon ground cayenne pepper (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 4 large flatbread wraps or pita bread
  • Sliced vegetables (lettuce, tomatoes, onions, cucumber)
  • Mayonnaise or garlic sauce
  • Ketchup or hot sauce (optional)

Instructions:

  • In a bowl, combine all the ingredients for the Suya marinade, except the beef. Mix well until you have a thick paste.
  • Add the thinly sliced beef into the marinade and coat the meat thoroughly. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour (or longer for more flavor) in the refrigerator.
  • Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. If using a grill pan, lightly grease it with oil to prevent sticking.
  • Thread the marinated beef slices onto skewers or place them directly on the grill. Cook the beef for about 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until cooked through and slightly charred. Remove from the heat and set aside.
  • Warm the flatbread wraps or pita bread in a pan or oven for a few seconds to make them pliable.
  • Assemble the shawarma by spreading a layer of mayonnaise or garlic sauce on each flatbread. Add a layer of sliced vegetables (lettuce, tomatoes, onions, cucumber) on top.
  • Place a few pieces of the grilled Suya beef on the vegetables. Drizzle with ketchup or hot sauce if desired.
  • Roll the flatbread tightly, folding in the sides as you go, to form a wrap. You can secure it with foil or parchment paper if needed.
  • Repeat the process for the remaining wraps.
  • Slice the wraps diagonally into halves or quarters.
  • Serve the Nigerian Suya Shawarma wraps as a delicious and spicy meal or snack.
